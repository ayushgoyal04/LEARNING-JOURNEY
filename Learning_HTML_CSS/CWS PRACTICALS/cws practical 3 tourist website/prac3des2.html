DIKSHA BHOOMI



Deekshabhoomi is a sacred monument of Navayana Buddhism located at Nagpur city in Maharashtra
state of India, where Dr. B. R. Ambedkar,[1] embraced Buddhism with approximately 600,000 of his
followers mainly scheduled caste peoples on Ashoka Vijaya Dashami[2] on 14 October 1956.[3][4]
Ambedkar revived Buddhism in India. Ambedkar's conversion to Buddhism is deeply significant for
millions of people in India.[5] According to the 2011 India census, more than 87% of the total
Buddhist population in India are Ambedkarite Buddhists
